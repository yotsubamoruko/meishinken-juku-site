# RIVA CAFE Research Notes

## Reference

- Source: `https://www.amepla.jp/f/amepla-cafe`

## What stood out

- Hero: short, confident headline and a strong visual lead.
- Message: one clear promise, repeated with softer copy below.
- Menu: three-way category framing that makes the offer easy to scan.
- Scenes: lunch, cafe, dinner are separated by use case and time of day.
- Space: seats, terrace, and private-room-like options are explained early.
- Access: station proximity, hours, and payment notes are surfaced plainly.
- FAQ: practical concerns are handled before the reservation CTA.

## Content patterns to borrow

- Keep the first screen light and editorial.
- Use short sections with one idea each.
- Repeat the same promise in slightly different words for recall.
- Show menu examples as easy-to-scan lists, not dense paragraphs.
- Make the space feel usable, not just pretty.

## Design implications for the fictional cafe

- Palette: cream, olive, terracotta, espresso.
- Typography: serif headlines, clean sans body.
- Motion: gentle reveals, floating art, marquee keyword strip.
- Visual language: bright, airy, handmade, not corporate.
- Layout: hero > concept > menu > space > FAQ > access.

## Fictional setting used

- Name: RIVA CAFE / TRATTORIA
- Style: casual stylish Italian cafe
- Location concept: Nagoya Station West Exit area
- Use cases: lunch, cafe break, light dinner, natural wine
